Beautiful Mallorca

Palma, or Palma de Mallorca as it is sometimes called, is not only the capital and largest city of Mallorca but also the entire Balearic archipelago.

"I live where I want to live. I live in Mallorca, and I'm not sure there's a better place in the world"
— Rafael Nadal.

For most people, Mallorca likely conjures up images of sunny beaches and lively parties. However, it is also an island rich in history, stunning landscapes, and remarkable nature.

It’s undoubtedly a fantastic beach destination, and there’s nothing wrong with coming here solely to relax by the sea. But if the spirit of adventure takes hold and you want to mix a bit of excitement with your leisure, Mallorca is full of thrilling secrets and delightful discoveries.

In many ways, Palma embodies a different, authentic Mallorca, one that stretches deep into the past.

Founded by the Romans centuries ago, Palma de Mallorca was long under Arab and Byzantine influence. To this day, the extraordinary blend of diverse cultures, religions, and lifestyles can be felt in the narrow streets of the old town.
